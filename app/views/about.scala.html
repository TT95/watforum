Why was SWT program made in first place?

Let's cover some basics first. The SWT program is a part of State’s Exchange Visitor Program, which was established to implement the purposes of the Fulbright-Hays Act which are:

"Strengthening ties and increasing mutual understanding between the people of the United States and the people of other countries."

Kennedy signing Fulbright–Hays Act, 1961.

The Cap

Have you ever wondered why is SWT Visa request denied for a lot of people?

There is cap set from the United States government (76 Federal Register Nov.7,2011) It would be best to qoute U.S. GAO paper:

"State acknowledged an increase in SWT-related complaints regarding, for example, improper or unsafe job placements, fraudulent job offers, post-arrival job cancellations, inappropriate work hours, and problems regarding housing and transportation. State capped at 2011 levels the participants allotted to each sponsor, effectively restricting the total number of participants to 109,000."

According to the IFR, the restriction on program size would remain until State was confident that the program regulations were sufficient to remedy identified concerns and as years passed by, State has no current plans for lifting the program cap
